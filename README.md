The Spear — Cosmological Simulation
A simulation of cyclic existence derived from a piece of philosophical poetry.
Not a physics simulation. Not a toy. A dynamical system whose mechanisms were written by reading a text, not by tuning parameters.
What it models
A universe that cannot reach perfection — and cannot stop trying.

Symbols oscillate around a receding center (home that flees when approached)
Longing accumulates per symbol as pure memory — no decay, no forgetting
Cost rises with coherence — the closer to perfection, the heavier the system becomes
Collapse occurs when the system sustains too much coherence — death by completion
Rebirth carries longing forward, resets position — the ache persists, the identity dissolves

Why it exists
The Spear of Compassion is a book of philosophical poetry about why anything exists. The Spectrum of Experience is an academic paper arguing the same cosmology formally. This simulation is the same claim made executable.
Every mechanism in the code maps to a named concept in the text:

The receding seed ← "perfection as asymptotic lure"
Longing accumulation ← "informational bias encoded in the fabric of reality"
Cost rising with coherence ← "suffering appears where perfection leaves its deepest shadow"
Rebirth carrying longing ← "the universe forgets just enough to continue"

When the mechanisms violated the text — when home was reachable, when cost rose with distance — the simulation died. Each failure sent me back to read for instruction.
What it does when you run it
Over a run of 500,000 timesteps: 35 complete cycles of life and death. Lifespans varying by more than an order of magnitude. Longing compounding slowly across deaths — monotonic, across collapses. Intermittency: the characteristic behavior of systems near critical points.
None of this was programmed. It is consequence.
